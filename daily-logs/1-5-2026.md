# Daily Dev Log: May 1, 2026

**Focus:** Codebase Analysis & Architectural Mapping (Puck to Shopify MVP)

## Overview
Today, I focused on studying and reverse-engineering the architecture of our split-screen MVP. The goal was to deeply understand how the codebase bridges the gap between our visual editor (React/CSR) and the target environment (Shopify/SSR). 

## What I Studied & Mapped
*   **Folder Architecture:** Mapped the separation of concerns across the application, specifically how the `editor/` handles authoring while the `core/` pipeline handles HTML compilation.
*   **The Serialization Engine:** Analyzed how `ReactDOMServer.renderToString()` is used in `renderer.tsx` to mathematically convert React component nodes into static HTML strings without needing a browser window.
*   **Performance Optimization:** Studied the implementation of the `useDebounce` hook in `App.tsx` and the `useMemo` hook in the preview component. I now understand how these protect the browser from crashing during heavy, real-time HTML rendering.
*   **Security & Data Flow:** Traced the JSON payload's journey from Puck's raw output, through the `transformer.ts` adapter, into the HTML string, and finally through the `DOMPurify` sanitizer to prevent XSS attacks.
