# Daily Dev Log: May 3, 2026

**Engineers:** Karim Hammad & Mansour  
**Focus:** Visual Editor Prototype & Backend Architecture Planning

## Overview
Today, Mansour and I held a meeting to research and plan the architecture for our visual editor prototype. We shifted our focus to an HTML-first, Server-Side Rendering (SSR) approach with a Rust backend. This change in direction required us to rethink the tools we are using.

## Key Discussions & Findings

*   **Puck Editor is Out:** We concluded that Puck is no longer an option for this prototype. Puck relies heavily on Client-Side Rendering (React), which conflicts with our new HTML-first, SSR goal.
*   **GrapesJS Alternative:** We researched GrapesJS as a potential replacement for building the visual editor. We made a list comparing its main advantages and disadvantages for our specific needs.
*   **Tera Templates (Rust Backend):** Since we decided to use Rust for the backend, we explored using Tera templates. Tera works very much like Shopify's Liquid templates, making it a strong option for rendering our HTML pages directly from the server.

## Next Steps
*   Review the pros and cons of GrapesJS vs. Tera templates as a team.
*   Select the final toolset so we can begin coding the new prototype.
